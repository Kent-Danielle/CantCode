Breadventory

A web app for managing the inventory and ordering process of bread in Langley Farm Market’s Deli Department made using the MERN stack and Chakra UI for customization.

Features

Automatic Ordering Sheet

To complete an order sheet, employees have to first check what breads are good or bad selling, then cross-reference it to yesterday’s order before doing the actual order sheet. Breadventory solves this by coming up an initial order sheet base on the sale status of the breads, saving the user a handful of trips around the store.

Bread Managing Capabilities

Breadventory allows the users to add and delete breads on the database, as well as being able to edit existing breads. Our suppliers often changes their stocks and promotions, and this feature allows Breadventory to be flexible in any real-world changes.

BuddyUp

A chatroulette for gamers that has a profile feature where users can post their gaming stories. In other words, Tinder but for gamers!

Features

Gamer Profile

Gamers can post their gaming stories in their profile page and other gamers can see it when they matched. Aside from their login credentials, gamers can also add what games they play to increase the chance of a good match!

Match ‘n Chat

Before proceeding to the chatroom, gamers may add or remove games on their “game interests” before going to the matching phase. When matched with another gamer, they will be able to see each other ‘s profile and can choose to refuse or accept the match.

FindaPotty

A Yelp - inspired web app for sharing, rating, and reviewing washroom locations based on the theme of “Winter Olympics at Vancouver”, powered by Firebase and designed with Bootstrap 5.

Features

Post-a-Potty

Users can share and rate a potty location along with a description of its features, and how can it be located from a certain landmark.

Rate-a-Potty

Users can also add ratings and reviews to other potties by clicking a potty location and navigating to the reviews tab.

Atlas

Atlas is a wearable tracking device powered by Arduino and a GSM module that can send its wearer's coordinates to the registered phone number with a click of a button.

Alternatively, the registered phone number can also send an SMS to Atlas, and Atlas will reply with the wearer's coordinates.

It is a product of my research in high school for helping elderlies, especially those that has memory issues and requires surveillance, by making a low cost tracker that can be paired with a phone number.
